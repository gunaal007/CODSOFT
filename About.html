Welcome to ABIDAT



Our Mission is to:

• Promote Balanced Learning and Scholarship: Foster an environment that encourages critical observation, discovery, and the acquisition of knowledge, skills, and values.

• Encourage Creativity and Innovation: Empower learners to creatively tackle the challenges of dryland agriculture, enhancing the quality of life for all stakeholders.

• Support Dynamic Research-Oriented Faculty: Ensure faculty are dedicated to teaching and mentoring, fulfilling academic and professional passions.

• Maintain Excellence and Quality Standards: Strive for excellence and quality across all activities, applying solutions on state, national, and international levels.

• Facilitate Academic and Cultural Exchange: Offer opportunities for study, research, and service both off-campus and overseas to scholars from diverse countries.

• Uphold Global Sustainability: Advocate for a pluralistic, multiracial, open, and democratic society through policies aimed at global sustainability.

• Encourage Interdisciplinary Studies: Generate new knowledge and foster creative endeavors through an ecosystem of innovation.

Our Vision

We believe people in water-scarce regions deserve the right to manage their land in a risk-mitigated environment. By rejuvenating fragile dryland and desert ecosystems sustainably and holistically, we aim to offer these communities opportunities for prosperous, dignified lives.


Our Values

We believe technology can level the playing field. By focusing on regions where agriculture relies on rainfall, we aim to improve the quality and productivity of these ecosystems through innovative technology and scientific methods, thereby enhancing the standard of living for marginal farmers.


Our Approach

Our approach combines scientific principles with appropriate technologies for drylands, driven by a passion for extending these innovations to farmers and communities. We employ a three-pronged strategy: identifying the problem, developing the solution, and implementing the solution, all driven by demand.


Parternship

ABIDAT is a collaboration between the ABAN Group of India and Ben Gurion University of the Negev (BGU), Israel, focusing on developing sustainable solutions for energy and food security. The PG Certificate in Dryland Studies program, offered through ABIDAT, features course content delivered by specialist professors from the Jacob Blaustein Institutes for Desert Research (BIDR) and the Albert Katz International School of Desert Studies (AKIS). Ben Gurion University of the Negev (BGU) is a global leader in desert agriculture and water-related research. Established to ensure Israel's food security, BGU is now a major exporter of agricultural products. Recently ranked 46th globally for sustainable policies, BGU promotes environmental protection, resource reduction, energy efficiency, and extensive educational and community activities. It also ranks 1st in "Student Satisfaction" among all academic institutions in Israel.

Diverse and Inclusive Education

We celebrate diversity in perspectives, cultures, and ideas. Our diverse course offerings cater to various interests and skill levels, ensuring that everyone finds a learning path that resonates with them.


Continuous Improvement

We are always evolving. Feedback from our learners helps us continually enhance our platform, ensuring it remains dynamic, responsive, and aligned with the evolving needs of our users.

Thank you! for being a part of ABIDAT. Together, let's embark on a journey of lifelong learning and growth.
